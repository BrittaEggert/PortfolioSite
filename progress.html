PROJECT DESCRIPTION

PROgress is a web application specially made for women to get advice from experienced women within the fitness industry.
The advanced expert knowledge optional via chat, phone call, or video call helps the women to reach their goals faster.

THE PROBLEM

Women need a way to get advanced knowledge from fitness experts to get in shape
because they would make just a little progress if they don't know what works best and would quickly lose motivation.

Picture: Becca Tappert - Unsplash

THE POTENTIAL SOLUTION

This app will allow users to get advanced expert knowledge around the clock.
They will get specific answers to their question and as well detailed information
right away if further questions pop up during the video chat consultation.

THE HYPOTHESIS STATEMENT

We believe that by encouraging women to get help from experts and therefore to learn new habits, they will gain their fitness goals faster.
We will know this be true when 30% of users reach their fitness goals within the first three months.

THE RESULTS

Both, the interviews and the survey gave me a deeper insight into my target group's behaviors
and goals. It was a challenge to reach enough participants when you have a narrow target group like
I chose to create an app for. But both research methods were well chosen for this type of app and
complemented each other well. In the end, I got some useful ideas that would convince users to use PROgress.

Three personas were created, based on the insights I gathered during the tests and a survey.
Each persona represents an entire audience of users. The product team can refer to this audience
by name during meetings, presentations, and other day-to-day work. These personas help to establish
empathy with users and help prioritize functionality based on audiences. Furthermore allow me as
a designer to present my research analysis in an engaging and informative way.

THE USER JOURNEYS

A user journey is an experience that the persona has when interacting with the application.
It provides an invaluable asset for the team as they advance through the user-centered design process.
The user journey is based the previous research and describes what steps different users take to
complete a specific task within an application or website. User journeys are focused on the user
and what they see and what they do. Creating a user journey help learn a lot about your user and product.

THE USER FLOWS

The user flow lays out the user's movement through the product, mapping out each and every step
the user takes - from the entry point right through to the final interaction.

THE SITEMAP

The sitemap is a hierarchical diagram of a website or application, that shows how pages are prioritized,
linked, and labeled.

THE USABILITY TEST

With clickable high-fidelity wireframes, a usability test was conducted. Six women participated.
A usability test reveals how a typical user would attempt to complete a task using your product.
During the test, you can observe the ability of participants to complete tasks, how long it takes
participants to complete tasks, whether participants enjoy interacting with the product or service
and whether the product or prototype has any critical errors. The test objectives were to determine
if participants understand what the app is about quick and easy and the value it provides.
To observe how users navigate and get the information they are looking for. And to observe if users
can easily solve basic tasks like sign up, book an appointment, and chat with an expert.

THE INSIGHTS

All the information was gathered in an infinity map (show infinity map) and then sorted by importance in a rainbow spreadsheet.
On this base, recommendations were made, the design was defined and another round of prototype testing started.
In another round, got valuable insights from other students, who tested and made notes on the prototype.
I followed some of the feedback and cleaned up the final screens and prototype for this iteration.
